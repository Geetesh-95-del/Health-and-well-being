Get Plenty of Sleep

Adequate sleep is vital for cognitive function, emotional health, and physical repair. Consistently aiming for 7-9 hours per night supports memory consolidation, hormonal balance, and strengthens your immune system.

Sleep is not a luxury, but a biological necessity that allows the body and brain to recover. During deep sleep, the body performs essential repair work, while the brain processes information and stores memories. Chronic sleep deprivation impairs judgment, reduces concentration, and increases susceptibility to stress and illness. Establishing a consistent sleep schedule and creating a cool, dark, and quiet environment are critical components of good sleep hygiene. Avoiding screens and caffeine close to bedtime also prepares the mind for rest, ensuring you wake up feeling refreshed and ready to engage with the world.
